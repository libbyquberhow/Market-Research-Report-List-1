<p><p>The anti-bleeding drugs market encompasses several types of hemostatic agents, including collagen, oxidized regenerated cellulose, gelatin, and polysaccharides. Collagen promotes platelet aggregation, aiding in clot formation. Oxidized regenerated cellulose acts as a physical barrier to bleeding, facilitating tissue healing. Gelatin, derived from collagen, enhances hemostasis by absorbing serum and promoting clotting. Polysaccharides, often found in plant-based agents, enhance coagulation through various mechanisms. Each type serves unique applications in surgical and emergency settings to control bleeding effectively.</p></p>
