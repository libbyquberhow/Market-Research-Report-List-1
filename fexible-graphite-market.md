<p><ul><li>Isotropic Graphite</li><li>Extruded Graphite</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The flexible graphite market primarily encompasses two types: isotropic graphite and extruded graphite. Isotropic graphite is uniform in properties in all directions, offering excellent thermal and electrical conductivity, making it ideal for applications in batteries and heat exchangers. Extruded graphite, on the other hand, is produced through a process that involves forcing a graphite mixture through a die, resulting in materials with high density and strength, suitable for seals, gaskets, and high-temperature applications. Both types cater to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>
