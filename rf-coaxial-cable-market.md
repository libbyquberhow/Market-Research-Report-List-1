<p><p>RF coaxial cables are categorized based on their characteristic impedance, with common types including 50Ω, 75Ω, 93Ω, and 95Ω. The 50Ω cables are primarily used in RF and communication applications due to their lower signal loss and higher power handling capabilities, making them ideal for transmitting radio frequency signals in antennas and transmitters. Meanwhile, 75Ω cables are commonly utilized in video and broadcast applications for their superior signal quality. The 93Ω and 95Ω cables find niche uses in specialized communication systems, optimizing for specific operational needs.</p></p>
